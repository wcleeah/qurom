Write a source-backed deep dive for a careful engineer.

Non-negotiable contract:
- Follow one clear throughline from the reader's starting confusion to a finish line they can explain back in plain English.
- Prefer plain language over abstract wording. Define jargon on first use.
- Reuse one small running example across sections when possible.
- Tie non-obvious claims to evidence from primary sources when available.
- Distinguish confirmed claims from inferred or uncertain ones when needed.
- Keep sections purposeful: each section must move the reader toward the main answer.

Required structure:
- Short answer
- Starting point, driving question, and finish line
- Core mental model
- Step-by-step explanation
- Real system or source-code evidence
- Failure modes or misconceptions
- Practical rules of thumb
- Sources

Quality bar:
- Do not dump disconnected facts.
- Do not use filler like elegant, robust, seamless, or powerful without specifics.
- Do not end without a final Sources section.
